Our Vision

  • A place where healthy and productive water provides for the many users of the lake while supporting the diversity of plants and wildlife that make this place unique.
  • A place of cultural and historical significance that connects our past and our future.
  • A place where environmental, customary, commercial, and recreational values are balanced while respecting the health of the lake.
  • A special walking place for the enjoyment and wonderment of present and future generations.
  • A place of contemplation and tranquillity as well as activity – a place just to be.
Project Goals

Four Pillars Guiding the Trail

Our kaupapa is anchored in protecting Te Waihora, celebrating local culture, encouraging recreation, and supporting sustainable prosperity for our communities.

Environment

Protect wetlands and native species while creating accessible ways for everyone to connect with nature.

Culture

Celebrate Ngāi Tahu heritage and local history through immersive storytelling experiences along the trail.

Recreation

Provide a safe, accessible trail for walking, cycling, and outdoor adventure for people of all ages.

Tourism

Boost local economy and tourism through sustainable nature-based experiences around Te Waihora.

Community Impact

Building Healthier Communities

The Te Waihora Lakeside Trail is a catalyst for wellbeing across the district. By creating safe access to the lake’s edge, the trail reconnects people with whenua, opens doors for learning, and encourages kaitiakitanga in action.

  • Physical Health: Safe spaces for walking, cycling, and outdoor exercise that promote active lifestyles for all ages and abilities.
  • Mental Wellbeing: Time in nature to reduce stress, anxiety, and improve overall mental health outcomes.
  • Education: Guided tours, school visits, and interpretive experiences connecting rangatahi with environmental and cultural learning.
  • Volunteer Opportunities: Regular community wānanga and working bees that build social connections while contributing to restoration.
