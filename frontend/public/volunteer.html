Lend a hand to Te Waihora

Why it matters

  • Hands-on restoration: Planting, weed control, predator monitoring, and trail care.
  • Learn & share: Build ecological knowledge alongside mana whenua and local experts.
  • Community connections: Meet people who love the lake and bring whānau along for the mahi.

Hands-on conservation

Help with planting days, track maintenance, monitoring traps, and lake surveys. Training and gear are provided – bring enthusiasm.

Connect with community

Work alongside mana whenua, local kaitiaki, and fellow supporters. Volunteers are the heartbeat of the trail.

Grow your skills

Learn wetland ecology, visitor hosting, and event coordination. Hours can count toward Duke of Ed or professional development.
