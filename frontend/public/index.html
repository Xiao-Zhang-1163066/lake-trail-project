Te Waihora Lake Ellesmere

Te Waihora Lake Ellesmere, one of New Zealand’s most important wetland systems, is a brackish, shallow lagoon with an average depth of 1.4 m. Covering around 20,000 hectares, Te Waihora Lake Ellesmere sits just to the south of Banks Peninsula, a taonga of immense biodiversity, cultural heritage, and community value.

It is internationally significant for its wildlife habitats, with at least 37 bird species breeding and 43 species of fish recorded in the lake. The total number of bird species recorded to date is 167.
